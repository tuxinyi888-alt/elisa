# Results: Effect of Carprofen on BCG Growth

## BCG growth assay by flow cytometry

To assess the effect of carprofen on mycobacterial growth, macrophages from four donors were infected with BCG-dsRed at three multiplicities of infection (MOI: B1, B10, B100) in the presence of increasing concentrations of carprofen (0, 1, 10, 100 µM). BCG growth was quantified by flow cytometry at 4 hours post-infection (4 hpi, baseline) and 7 days post-infection (7 dpi). Integrated fluorescence (IF) was calculated as the product of BCG event counts and the geometric mean of PE-A fluorescence intensity, normalised to bead counts. Fold change in IF (7 dpi / 4 hpi) was used as the primary readout of BCG replication over time. Uninfected controls (B0) were included to account for background fluorescence.

### Uninfected controls (B0)

In the absence of BCG infection, fold change values remained close to 1.0 for both extracellular (EC: 1.02–1.69) and intracellular (IC: 0.92–1.27) fractions across all carprofen concentrations, confirming minimal background fluorescence change over the 7-day culture period.

### Extracellular BCG (EC)

At low MOI (B1), extracellular BCG fold change was modest across all conditions (range: 1.11–1.67), with no clear dose-dependent effect of carprofen.

At intermediate MOI (B10), a dose-dependent reduction in extracellular BCG growth was observed with increasing carprofen concentration. The mean fold change decreased from 4.18 ± 1.16 (C0, vehicle control) to 3.34 ± 0.99 (C1), 2.57 ± 0.62 (C10), and 1.55 ± 0.27 (C100) (mean ± SEM, n = 4). This represented an approximately 2.7-fold reduction in BCG growth at the highest carprofen concentration relative to untreated controls.

At high MOI (B100), a similar trend was apparent, with the highest carprofen concentration (C100) yielding a markedly lower fold change (3.73 ± 1.17) compared to the vehicle control (11.30 ± 5.67). However, substantial inter-donor variability was observed in this group, particularly at C0 and C1, limiting the strength of this comparison.

### Intracellular BCG (IC)

In contrast to the extracellular fraction, intracellular BCG fold change values remained relatively low and stable across all MOIs and carprofen concentrations (range: 0.90–2.12). No consistent dose-dependent effect of carprofen was observed on intracellular BCG growth. At B10, there was a modest increase in intracellular fold change with higher carprofen concentrations (C0: 1.22 ± 0.20 to C100: 2.12 ± 0.79), though this trend was not observed at other MOIs and was associated with high variability.

### Summary

These data suggest that carprofen inhibits extracellular BCG growth in a dose-dependent manner, most clearly demonstrated at MOI B10. This effect was not mirrored in the intracellular compartment, where BCG replication remained largely unchanged regardless of carprofen treatment. The discrepancy between extracellular and intracellular responses may reflect differences in drug accessibility or the distinct microenvironments of extracellular versus phagosomal BCG. Further investigation with additional donors and statistical testing would be needed to confirm these observations.
